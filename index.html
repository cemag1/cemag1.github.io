Bio

Fig.2 Graduations are fun.

Hey! I am a PhD student at the University of Cambridge, working with the Computer-aided Manufacturing group of the Engineering Department. Previously, I did my MSc in artificial intelligence at Imperial College London as well as a BEng in mechanical engineering at University of Birmingham. My research interest lies on fusing engineering and AI, to enable 'Learning 3D printers' - systems with the capacity to grasp the notion of their own underlying principles and engage in self-improvement to manufacture better and stronger parts. I am also interested in how we can make these systems leverage knowledge pertaining to one AM method to improve another, a simple task for humans but currently impossible for AI.

I had some friction with industry through a number of placements, including one with the Materials Science Division of LLNL in California and one with the Security Division of IBM in London.

During my academic journey I had the honour to receive a few awards: an EPSRC Scolarship, an IMechE award, the Ellis, Linning, and Sandifer prize, and others.
